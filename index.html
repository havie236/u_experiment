Instructions

The experiment consists of two parts. Please start by reading the explanations for the first part carefully. You will receive the instructions for the second part of the experiment after the first part is finished. If you have any questions during the experiment please ask the experimenter.

In this experiment, your task is to count zeros in a series of tables.

Enter the number of zeros into the box on the screen. After you have entered the number, click the OK button. If you enter the correct result, a new table will be generated.

If your input was wrong, you have two additional tries to enter the correct number into the table. You therefore have a total of three tries to solve each table.

Rules for Part 1:
  • You have exactly 4 minutes to count as many tables as possible.
  • You receive 400 VND for each table you solve correctly.
  • If you enter three times a wrong number for a table, 400 VND will be subtracted from your earnings and a new table will then be generated.

Debriefing Statement

Thank you for participating in this study.

The purpose of this experiment is to understand how simply seeing information about a peer’s earnings influences your effort in a task.

During the experiment, you may have seen information about the average earnings of previous participants. This information was intentionally varied (higher or lower benchmark) across different participants to study how different reference points affect work behavior.

The benchmark numbers are used only for research purposes and did not affect your payment rate. Your actual earnings are calculated purely based on your own performance.

Thank you again for your time and contribution to this research!
